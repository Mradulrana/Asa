Medical practitioners are faced with common errors and challenges in infectious disease billing. Below are some of them with specific ways to avoid them:

Up and Down Coding

Upcoding refers to the act of billing for a more expensive treatment or procedure than what was performed, while on the other hand, down coding is filing a claim for a less expensive procedure. Both of these are illegal and could lead to charges of fraud. To avoid such instances, billing practices must partner with third-party billing companies to ensure services rendered match the submitted codes.

Incomplete Documentation

Incorrect or incomplete documentation leads to claims denials, which result in delayed payments. To curb this, it is essential to ensure all relevant information is included in the medical records, supporting the relevant billing codes.

Verifying Insurance Coverage

Failure in insurance coverage verification results in loss of revenue through denied claims. To avoid this, it is imperative to run insurance coverage verification before offering treatment or services. It is also vital to update patients’ insurance data regularly.

Unbundling for Billing

Unbundling refers to billing for every component of a bundled-up procedure independently; while this results in higher reimbursement rates, it is illegal. This illegality shall lead to fraud charges. It is essential to bill for bundled procedures through a single code to avoid such instances. Additionally, it is important that the services offered match the submitted codes.
